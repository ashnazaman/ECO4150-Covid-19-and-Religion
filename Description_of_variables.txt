Ethnic heterogeneity and covid: description of variables


Epidemiological profile:

fairorpoorhealth: Percentage of adults reporting fair or poor health (age-adjusted).*

smokers: Percentage of adults who are current smokers.*

Adultswithobesity: Percentage of the adult population (age 20 and older) that reports a body mass index (BMI) greater than or equal to 30 kg/m2.*

Physicallyinactive: Percentage of adults age 20 and over reporting no leisure-time physical activity.*

Withaccesstoexerciseopportunitie: Percentage of population with adequate access to locations for physical activity.*

Excessivedrinking: Percentage of adults reporting binge or heavy drinking.*

Chlamydiarate: Number of newly diagnosed chlamydia cases per 100,000 population.*

Vaccinated: Percentage of fee-for-service (FFS) Medicare enrollees that had an annual flu vaccination.*

lifeexpectancy: Average number of years a person can expect to live.*

Adultswithdiabetes: Percentage of adults aged 20 and above with diagnosed diabetes.*

Hivprevalencerate: Number of people aged 13 years and older living with a diagnosis of human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection per 100,000 population.*

Insufficientsleep: Percentage of adults who report fewer than 7 hours of sleep on average.*















Socioeconomic characteristics:

uninsuredadults: Percentage of adults under age 65 without health insurance.*
Poverty: Percentage of poverty.
disconnectedyouth: Percentage of teens and young adults ages 16-19 who are neither working nor in school.*

Averagetrafficvolumepermeterofma: Average traffic volume per meter of major roadways in the county.*

Notproficientinenglish: Percentage of population that is not proficient in English.*
residentialsegregationblackwhite: Index of dissimilarity where higher values indicate greater residential segregation between Black and White county residents.*

Residentialsegregationnonwhitewh/seg_index: Index of dissimilarity where higher values indicate greater residential segregation between non-White and White county residents.*

density_2018: Density of population in a county
unemployed: Percentage of population ages 16 and older unemployed but seeking work.*
Socialassociationrate: Number of membership associations per 10,000 population.*
Overcrowding: Percentage of households with overcrowding.*
immigrants: Percentage of foreign-born population.
male: Percentage of male residents.
age: Average age of residents.
educ: Percentage of adults with at least a high school degree.
urban: Percentage of population living in an urban area.*
avgrad_reading: Average grade level performance for 3rd graders on English Language Arts standardized tests*

avgrad_math: Average grade level performance for 3rd graders on math standardized tests.*



average_occupancy_per_room: Average number of occupants per room (using the average of each intervals given by the Census Bureau).
occupancy_morethan1: Percentage of households having more than 1 occupants per room.
occupancy_morethan2: Percentage of households having more than 2 occupants per room.
workers_nonteleworking: Percentage of workers that can't work form home (estimations).


County health’s characteristics:

otherprimarycareproviderrate: Ratio of population to primary care providers other than physicians.*

healthworker: ratio of population to primary care providers.*

averagedailypm25: Average daily density of fine particulate matter in micrograms per cubic meter (PM2.5).*

hop: Number of hospitals in a county.

Lbeds: Number of licensed beds in a county. 

Sbeds: Number of staffed beds in a county. 

over65: Percentage of population ages 65 and older.*

inc: The income where half of households in a county earn more and half of households earn less.*

mean_inc: Mean income in a county.

Incineq: Income Inequality (ratio of mean on median income).

mask_never: The estimated share of people in this county who would say never in response to the question: 
“How often do you wear a mask in public when you expect to be within six feet of another person?”.**
mask_rarely: The estimated share of people in this county who would say rarely.**
mask_sometimes: The estimated share of people in this county who would say sometimes.**
mask_frequently: The estimated share of people in this county who would say frequently.**
mask_always: The estimated share of people in this county who would say always.**
mask_average: The average of mask use (scale of 0 to 4)



Governor’s caracteristics:

tenure: number of years as governor.

ageg: age of the governor.

educg: dummy if completed college (1 if so).

numbofdegree: 0, 1, 2 degrees from college.

lnumbofdeg_gov: log of numbofdegree.

sexeg: sex of the governor.

party: dummy (1 if a republican governor).

iregion: 1, 2, 3 or 4, Midwest, South, Northeast and West.



Races: 

EHI: Ethnic Heterogeneity Index.

lEHI: logarithm of EHI.

hisp: Percentage of population that is Hispanic.*

white: Percentage of population that is non-Hispanic White.*

black: Percentage of population that is Black.*

native: Percentage of population that is American Indian or Alaska Native.*

Asian: Percentage of population that is Asian.*

Pacisl: Percentage of population that is Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander.*

Other: Percentage of population that is other races.

twoormoreraces: Percentage of population that is two or more races.

Totalpopulation/pop: Resident population

hispanicorlatinoofanyrace: Number of residents that are Hispanic 

whitealone: Number of residents that are non-Hispanic White.

Blackorafricanamericanalone: Number of residents that are Black.

Americanindianandalaskanativealo: Number of residents that are American Indian or Alaska Native

asianalone: Number of residents that are Asian.

Nativehawaiianandotherpacificisl: Number of residents that are Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander.

Someotherracealone: Number of residents that are another race.








Interaction terms:

seg_EHI: interaction between EHI and seg_index
dens_EHI: interaction between EHI and density_2018.
ageg_EHI: interaction between EHI and ageg.
sexeg_EHI: interaction between EHI and sexeg
party_EHI: interaction between EHI and party
educg_EHI: interaction between lEHI and lnumbofdeg_gov

deaths_03_15: number of cumulative deaths until March 15.
deaths_04_01: number of cumulative deaths until April 1st.
deaths_04_15: number of cumulative deaths until April 15
deaths_05_01: number of cumulative deaths until May 1st.
deaths_05_15: number of cumulative deaths until May 15
deaths: number of cumulative deaths until June 1st.

deathrate_03_15: deathrate on March 15.
